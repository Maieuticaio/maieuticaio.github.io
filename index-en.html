Transitional Object

An immersive sculpture, a set of states and emotions designed for real-time mood tracking and maintaining an inner dialogue. Its purpose is to bring an experience outward into a physical object. Interaction with the sculpture slows perception down: it is not just a record of a state, but a tactile and spatial experience that makes it possible not to analyze things abstractly, but to see, touch, and relate them to bodily and visual experience. The sculpture acts as a mediator between different parts of the self, allowing a dialogue to begin while holding focus. It does not take over completely, but it also does not allow important displaced parts to be forgotten, becoming an anchor for them.

The task of transitional objects is to support a person in interacting with uncomfortable, displaced, or unexpressed states that often have no place in everyday life. They become a companion tool in the process of integration, giving different states form, place, and recognition, and helping restore inner wholeness.

In a sense, this is a mourning woman. Historically, a mourning woman was invited to express peak emotional states: feelings that the participants in a ritual could not always, or were not allowed to, express themselves. The sculptures continue this tradition, but translate it into the space of individual inner work.
